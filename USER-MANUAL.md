## Introduction

**ONA** is a comprehensive eye health screening application designed for community health workers and clinics. The app provides:

- ✅ **100% Offline Operation** - Works without internet connection
- 👁️ **Visual Acuity Testing** - Near vision screening using Tumbling E optotypes
- 🤖 **AI-Powered Eye Analysis** - On-device image analysis for risk assessment
- 🔒 **Privacy-First** - All data stored locally and encrypted
- 🌍 **Multi-Language Support** - English, Français, Kiswahili, Lingala

### Important Notice
⚠️ **This application is a SCREENING TOOL ONLY. It does NOT provide medical diagnosis or treatment. All results must be confirmed by a qualified healthcare professional.**

### Step 2: Visual Acuity Calibration

**Purpose:** Calibrate screen size for accurate optotype display.

**Screenshot Location:** Calibration Screen
- Credit card icon
- Dashed rectangle outline on screen
- Information box below

**Instructions:**
1. Find a standard bank/credit card (85.6mm × 53.98mm)
2. Place the card on your screen over the dashed rectangle
3. Ensure the card matches the rectangle size
4. Tap **"Card Placed - Continue"** when aligned

**Why This Matters:**
Proper calibration ensures optotypes (test letters) are displayed at the correct size for accurate vision testing.

**Screenshot Location:** Calibration Screen

### Step 3: Visual Acuity Test - Right Eye

**Purpose:** Test near vision acuity using Tumbling E optotypes.

**Screenshot Location:** VA Test Screen (Right Eye)
- Eye icon (blue/info color for right eye)
- Large "E" letter in center
- Direction buttons below

**Test Protocol:**

1. **Patient Preparation:**
   - Ask patient to cover their LEFT eye
   - Hold phone at 40cm distance
   - Ensure good lighting

2. **Test Display:**
   - Screen shows: "Cover Left Eye"
   - Current level displayed (e.g., "Level: 6/60")
   - Large "E" rotated in random direction

3. **Patient Response:**
   - Ask: "Which way does the E point?"
   - Patient indicates direction: ↑ Up / ↓ Down / ← Left / → Right

4. **Operator Input:**
   - Tap the direction button matching patient's answer
   - 4 direction buttons arranged in cross pattern:
     - ↑ Up (top)
     - ← Left (left) | → Right (right)
     - ↓ Down (bottom)

5. **Can't See Button:**
   - Gray button at bottom
   - Tap if patient cannot see the optotype
   - Completes test for this eye

**Test Logic:**
- Starts at largest size (6/60)
- Advances to smaller sizes on correct answers
- Requires 2 consecutive correct answers to advance
- 2 incorrect answers completes the test
- Automatically rotates E to new direction after each response

**Screenshot Location:** VA Test Active
- Header shows which eye to cover
- Level indicator
- Large rotated "E" letter
- 4 blue direction buttons in cross pattern
- Gray "Can't See" button
- Instructions at top

## Troubleshooting

### Camera Issues

**Problem:** Camera not working
**Solutions:**
1. Check camera permissions in device settings
2. Close and reopen the app
3. Restart your device
4. Ensure no other app is using camera

**Problem:** Poor image quality
**Solutions:**
1. Clean camera lens
2. Improve lighting conditions
3. Hold device steady
4. Ask patient to open eye wider
5. Retake photo

---

### Test Issues

**Problem:** Patient cannot see optotypes
**Solutions:**
1. Check calibration was done correctly
2. Improve lighting
3. Ask patient to move closer/farther
4. Use "Can't See" button to record
5. Note concerns in patient notes

**Problem:** Optotype not rotating
**Solutions:**
1. Wait for current rotation to complete
2. Ensure you tapped a direction button
3. If stuck, restart the screening

---

### Data Issues

**Problem:** Screening not saving
**Solutions:**
1. Check available storage space
2. Complete all required steps
3. Don't force quit during save
4. Try saving again from results screen

**Problem:** History not showing screenings
**Solutions:**
1. Ensure screenings were saved
2. Check if data was cleared
3. Restart app to refresh

**Problem:** Cannot print/share results
**Solutions:**
1. Check device storage permissions
2. Ensure sharing is available on device
3. Try again with stable system state
4. On web, allow pop-ups for print

---

### Language Issues

**Problem:** Wrong language displaying
**Solutions:**
1. Go to Settings
2. Select correct language
3. App updates immediately

**Problem:** Some text not translating
**Solutions:**
1. This may be expected for technical terms
2. Report to app administrators
3. Key medical info maintained for safety

---

### General Issues

**Problem:** App freezing or crashing
**Solutions:**
1. Force close and reopen app
2. Restart device
3. Check for app updates
4. Clear app cache (device settings)
5. Reinstall app if persistent

**Problem:** App running slowly
**Solutions:**
1. Close other apps
2. Clear device RAM
3. Ensure adequate storage space
4. Check device isn't overheating

## Appendix: Understanding Results

### Risk Categories Explained

**Low Risk (Green 🟢)**
- No immediate concerns detected
- Vision appears adequate
- Routine follow-up recommended
- Continue regular eye care

**Medium Risk (Yellow 🟡)**
- Some concerns detected
- May need professional evaluation
- Monitor for changes
- Schedule appointment within 1-2 months

**High Risk (Red 🔴)**
- Significant concerns detected
- URGENT professional evaluation needed
- Refer immediately to eye care professional
- Document concerns clearly

### What Results Mean

**Visual Acuity Scores:**
- Higher scores = Better vision
- Lower scores = More difficulty seeing
- Both eyes tested independently
- Score reflects smallest optotype seen

**AI Image Analysis:**
- Analyzes eye appearance
- Detects potential anomalies
- Quality score indicates image clarity
- Risk assessment based on patterns

**Overall Assessment:**
- Combines all test results
- Takes worst-case score
- Provides unified recommendation
- Guides referral decision
